Food Support

Ghana has not been self-sufficient in food production and distribution and it has been difficult to ensure food availability in sufficient quantities all year round and in all regions.
There are certain times that the food becomes available seasonally,　but most people cannot afford to buy them as a result of poverty.

Poverty is a major cause of hunger and malnutrition among people living in the rural areas despite government’s pro-poor programs and has made food insecurity in some parts of the country.
Therefore, As part of Monphy and Youth foundation’s programs in addressing food insecurity by augmenting government’s efforts in achieving zero hunger in rural areas has since 2011, distributed tones of rice, sugar, maize,mineral water, soap, cooking oil and baby foods to the aged, physically-challenged people, pregnant women and patients at various hospitals. 

During the distribution of the foods we realized that peoples livelihoods are in a terrible and miserable state. For example, A family of 5 depends on 2 dollars (equivalent to ¥200) a day and they even lack jobs to enable them have better lives.
Anytime I visit Ghana most children living around come to my house to request for food with their bowls in hand.
After giving what they asked for and looking at them going back to explains how they are really suffering with hunger. It saddens my heart and I pity them because when you compare them to the children living in Japan makes their situations unfortunate and it makes tears fills my eyes.

There are food available here in Japan thousand tone of food going wastage makes me ask you, what are we all doing to gather to bring down this disparity between the people here in Japan and that of the rural people in Ghana...
