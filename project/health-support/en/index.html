The reality of distribution of food stuff to the needy and the poor.

In Ghana there are certain times that food becomes available seasonally. Poverty is a major cause of hunger in the rural areas.
In 2011, Monphy & Youth NGO distributed tones of food such as rice, sugar, maize, mineral water, soap, cooking oil and baby foods to the aged, physically-challenged people, pregnant women and patients at various hospitals. During the distribution of the foods we realized that peoples livelihoods are in a terrible and miserable state.
For example, a family of 5 depends on 2 dollars (equivalent to ¥200) a day and they even lack of jobs. Anytime I visit Ghana most children living around come to my house to request for food with their bowls in hand, some find it difficult to even make a meal a day unlike Japanese children who are able to meet three square meals a day whilst thousand tones of surplus food are wasted and thrown away each year.
Such unfortunate situation saddens my heart and I pity them tears fills my eyes.

Some people have their happiness loving animals, some enjoy going out and eating with friends, some go for entertainments and games which bring Joy to them.
My also happiness is, when Pains, stress and suffering are taken out from children. When parents are taken out of poverty… It is in view of these that I am doing all I can do to put more smiles and happiness on their faces. The smiles and happiness of children  encourages me, gives me strength and is positively impacting on me. And is the reason you see me defying all odds to stand in these severe weather conditions raising funds to save lives especially young children through good feeding improving undernourishment and malnutrition, helping alleviating poverty from poor parents through education.

I’m therefore ask you all doing to gather to bring down this disparity between the people here in Japan and that of the rural people in Ghana...
kThank You for your warm kindness and cooperation .

Ambulance Presentation.

The rural areas or the villages where ambulance services are mostly needed are virtually unavailable.
This has made accessing health facilities in case of emergencies extremely difficult.

The situation at which people especially children, Pregnant women and the aged, were dying on their way to far-away hospitals, it was heartbreaking.
Monphy and Youth foundation made a passionate appeal to the Tokyo Metropolitan Government which they gave to us TWO ambulances and some hospital materials. We distributed the ambulances to Nkoranzaman Health Center and Regional Hospital at Sunyani, as these hospitals had no Ambulances. These ambulances are serving their intended purposes for helping transport emergency cases of children, pregnant women and accident victims in critical conditions to hospitals better equipped with medical experts in the big cities.

In emergency cases the hospitals have to rent a car to transport patients to the bigger hospitals and sometimes patients die because there are no paramedics to monitor them.
We were encouraged by the confessions of the Regional director that our efforts are yielding positive results in achieving our aims and objectives as an organization.
The community are thanking all who put in efforts for them to have the ambulances and indeed they are asking for more.
